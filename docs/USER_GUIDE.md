# Deepblock — User Guide

Deepblock is a daily deep work planner built on three frameworks:

- **Cal Newport — Deep Work**: Protect and measure focused, distraction-free work blocks.
- **James Clear — Atomic Habits**: Identity-based habit tracking with streak accountability.
- **Peter Drucker — The Effective Executive**: Ruthless prioritisation via the 3 Must-Do task and the Not-Doing list.

---

## Getting started

1. Sign in (or continue as a guest — guest data lives in your browser only).
2. On your first visit the onboarding tour runs automatically. You can restart it any time from the Help button.
3. When you open a fresh day you'll be prompted for your **bed time, wake time, and sleep target**. Your awake hours are divided into five time blocks automatically.

---

## Day planner

### Time blocks

Your day is split into five blocks that scale to your wake/sleep window:

| Block | Default window | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Morning routine | Wake → +2 h | Prep, exercise, review |
| High Priority | Morning end → ~5 PM | **Deep work** — protect this |
| Medium Priority | Afternoon | Important but not urgent |
| Low Priority | Late afternoon | Optional / nice-to-have |
| Night routine | Evening → sleep | Wind-down |

You can resize any block using the edit icon in the section header. Changes can be applied to today only or saved as your default template.

### Task sections

Each block contains a section. Add tasks with the input at the bottom of each section.

**3 Must-Do tasks** sit above the blocks as your non-negotiables for the day. Cal Newport's 4DX principle: focus on the wildly important.

### Task options (right-click or ⋮ menu)

| Action | What it does |
|---|---|
| Edit | Rename the task |
| Add task above / below | Insert a task at a precise position |
| Add subtask | Create a child task. Completing the parent checks all subtasks. |
| **Mark as shallow** | Flag as low-depth work (see Deep Work section below) |
| Move to Not Doing | Sends to the global Not-Doing list — a conscious decision to drop it |
| Abandon | Records it as consciously abandoned (Drucker: abandonment as success) |
| Delete | Remove permanently |

### Time and duration

Set a **scheduled time** (HH:MM) to get a due-now reminder — the task highlights and a short beep plays when the clock hits that time. Set a **duration** to feed the shallow-time budget and the completion score.

## Deep Work (Cal Newport)

### The timer

The deep work timer is in the **sidebar** on the right.

1. Pick a preset duration: 15, 25, 30, 45, 50, or 60 minutes (or type your own).
2. Add a label: what are you working on? ("German study", "Startup MVP", "Job apps").
3. Press **Start**.

When the countdown ends the session is **automatically saved** to your day. A **teal badge** in the day header shows your total deep work minutes for the day.

### Shallow task tracking

Every task is deep by default. Mark tasks as **shallow** (logistical, administrative, non-cognitively demanding) via the ⋮ menu.

- Shallow tasks show an **amber "shallow" badge**.
- When completed shallow tasks total **≥ 2 hours** in a day, a warning banner appears above your sections: *protect your deep blocks*.

This implements Newport's "drain the shallows" principle — keep shallow work to a fixed budget.

### Weekly scoreboard

In the **Tracking dashboard** (scroll down on the planner page):

- **Deep Work This Week**: a progress bar showing hours logged vs. your weekly goal.
- **Per-day bar chart**: Mon–Sun, each bar proportional to that day's deep work minutes.
- **Goal**: Click the goal number to edit it inline (default: 20 h/week). Syncs to your account.

This is the **lead measure scoreboard** from Newport's 4DX framework. Track the behavior (hours of deep work), not just the outcome (tasks completed).

### Depth philosophy

Also in the Tracking dashboard — choose your approach:

| Philosophy | What it means | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Rhythmic** | Same fixed block every day (e.g. 9 AM–12 PM is sacred) | Most people — sustainable, habit-forming |
| **Journalistic** | Insert deep work whenever a gap appears | Experienced practitioners only |
| **Bimodal** | Multi-day deep work periods, rest of the time shallow | Academic / seasonal schedules |

When **Rhythmic** is selected, a teal banner appears above the High Priority section in the planner showing your current deep block window. It's a visual reminder to protect that time.

## Atomic Habits (James Clear)

Three principles are built directly into the app.

### 1. Identity statement

At the top of the habit checklist in the sidebar: click the italic text to edit your **"I am X"** declaration — e.g. *"I am someone who does deep work every day"* or *"I am someone who shows up regardless of how I feel."*

Press Enter or click away to save. It syncs to your account and appears every day as the first thing you see in the checklist. Clear's argument: you don't rise to the level of your goals, you fall to the level of your identity.

### 2. Daily habit checklist

The **habit checklist** is in the right sidebar, collapsible with a click on "Daily Habits".

- Tap any habit to **check it off** for today (tap again to uncheck).
- Each habit shows a **🔥 N streak** — consecutive days it was completed.
- The header shows **N / total** completed today.

**Default habits** (used until you customise): Gym, Eat Clean, Pray, Bed Before 23:00, Journaling, Screen Time - 5h, NoFaps, Deep Work, Post 3x, Deep Learning.

### 3. Never-miss-twice alert

If a habit was **completed the day before yesterday** but **missed yesterday**, it is flagged today with an **amber highlight and ⚠ warning**: *"You missed yesterday — don't miss twice."*

This implements Clear's most important rule: missing once is an accident, missing twice is the start of a new (bad) habit. Check the habit today to break the pattern.

### Editing habits

Click **"Edit"** in the checklist header to open the habit editor.

| Action | How |
|---|---|
| Add a habit | Type name in the bottom input + Enter or click Add |
| Delete a habit | Click ✕ on the right of any habit row |
| Reorder | Click ▲ or ▼ next to the habit |
| Set a stack anchor | Type in the "After…" field below the habit name |

Changes take effect when you click **Save**. Habits sync to your account.

### Habit stacking

Each habit has an optional **"After…" anchor field** in the editor — e.g. *"After waking up"*, *"After lunch"*, *"After gym"*.

The anchor is shown as a small line below the habit name in the checklist: *"After: gym"*. This is the trigger phrase from Clear's habit-stacking formula: **"After [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."** Stacking attaches new behaviors to existing anchors so they fire automatically.

### Monthly habit grid

In the **Tracking dashboard** (scroll down on the planner page), below the block completion grid:

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Habit name | Truncated; hover for full name |
| Day 1–31 | ✓ = completed that day, · = not completed |
| 🔥 (last column) | Current streak count |

The grid shows the selected month. Use the ← → arrows at the top of the dashboard to browse past months. This is your consistency history — the visual proof that the system is working.

## Monthly Tracking Dashboard

Scroll down on the planner page to see the full tracking dashboard.

### Block completion grid

A month-at-a-glance table: each column is a day, each row is a section.

| Cell colour | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Green ✓ | All tasks in that section completed |
| Amber | Partial completion |
| Red | Tasks exist but none done |
| Empty · | No tasks that day |

### Mood

Click any day in the mood row to log how you felt. Eight options: 🙂 😐 🙁 😊 😢 😤 😴 🔥.

### Chapter title

Give each month a name — "The Foundation", "The Grind", "The Pivot". Shown at the top of the grid.

## Not-Doing list (Drucker)

The sidebar contains a **Not-Doing panel**.

- **Global list**: things you've decided to stop pursuing entirely (persists across all days).
- **Day list**: things you're consciously not doing today.

Add items manually, or move a task here via its ⋮ menu. Drucker's principle: making explicit what you won't do is as important as deciding what you will.

---

## Carrying tasks forward

**Carry forward incomplete tasks** — a button appears at the top of the day when yesterday had incomplete tasks. It imports them to today, deduplicates, and increments a postpone counter. Tasks postponed 3+ times show a ⚠ badge as a nudge.

**Fill from last [weekday]** — copies your recurring schedule from the same weekday last week. Best for weekly routines (Monday = gym + deep work + study).

**Copy from yesterday** — shown when no same-weekday data exists. Good for the first week.

## Sharing

Click **Share** in the top bar. Generate a link with:

- **View permission**: anyone can see your planner (read-only).
- **Edit permission**: anyone with the link can add and complete tasks.

Revoke access any time by generating a new link.

## Sync and data

| Mode | Where data lives | Cross-device |
|---|---|---|
| Guest | Browser localStorage only | No |
| Signed in | localStorage (cache) + Supabase cloud | Yes — automatic |

All data — tasks, deep work sessions, habit completions, philosophy settings, weekly goal — syncs automatically when signed in. Changes made on one device appear on others within seconds.

## Keyboard shortcuts and tips

- **Ctrl/Cmd + Click** a task to multi-select, then move or delete the batch.
- Right-click the drag grip to open the context menu on desktop.
- The timer keeps running if you navigate to another tab — come back when it finishes.
- The day completion progress bar in the header uses **duration-weighted scoring**: longer and higher-priority tasks count for more.
